We're back in the maze again! Last time you were here, you used <define>`move()`</define> function to move one step at a time. That worked, but it gets pretty tedious writing `move()` many times in a row!

In this exercise, we've replaced `move()` with a new <define>function</define> called <define>`walk()`</define>. This function takes a number as its <define>input</define> and moves forward that many steps. For example, `walk(3)` moves forward 3 steps, and `walk(1)` moves forward just 1 step.

In all future exercises, you'll have a "Functions" section like the one below that makes it easy for you to see exactly what functions you have available and how they work.

Use `walk()`, <define>`turnLeft()`</define>, and <define>`turnRight()`</define> to navigate through the maze and reach the green target cell.
